Historic Edinburgh

Embarking on a tour of Historic Edinburgh unveils a tapestry of tales woven into the very fabric of the city.

The majestic Edinburgh Castle, perched atop Castle Rock, serves as a sentinel echoing centuries of Scottish history. From its origins in the 12th century to its role in pivotal conflicts, the castle's stone walls harbor stories of kings and battles. Delving into its architecture reveals a blend of medieval and Renaissance styles, with the Crown Square housing must-see exhibits like the Crown Jewels and the Royal Palace showcasing regal opulence. This historic journey through Edinburgh offers a captivating glimpse into the rich tapestry of the city's cultural and architectural heritage. Descending from the grandeur of Edinburgh Castle, the Royal Mile stretches down the historic heart of the city, connecting the fortress to the Palace of Holyroodhouse. This iconic thoroughfare, measuring approximately one Scots mile, or 1.12 miles, is a bustling boulevard steeped in tradition and lined with a medley of shops, cafes, and landmarks. Cobblestone streets lead visitors through a living museum of Scottish history, with each step revealing centuries of stories etched into the very fabric of the Royal Mile.
